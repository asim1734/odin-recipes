Gulab Jamun

Description

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from milk solids, traditionally from khoya. These deep-fried dough balls are soaked in sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water. A delicious treat often enjoyed during festivals and celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (dried milk solids)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • Oil or ghee for frying
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp rose water

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add milk gradually to form a smooth dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls and make sure there are no cracks.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and fry the dough balls on medium heat until golden brown.
  4. In another pan, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, cardamom powder, and rose water together.
  5. Soak the fried gulab jamuns in warm syrup for at least 1 hour before serving.
